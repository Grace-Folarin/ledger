Managing the grid, stripping out cost

Our platform is making a big difference

The central problems in electrical generation are high supply during the day and high demand in the evening. This is often called the duck curve

You can store that problem away with hydro or batteries and you can trade it away. But you can’t ignore it.

Renewables are the solution and the problem

Midday oversupply of solar energy plays havoc with the grid and creates reverse flows to distribution networks. This can burnout transformers, cause harmonics and voltage outages and actually requires even more equipment to stabilize the grid.

Oversupply puts more demand on grid ancillary services - which relate to frequency stability - and because these services are expensive, these costs go up.

All of this activity makes it harder for the baseload generation to happen efficiently and costs go up too.

These issues are being experienced in every market with high penetration of renewables - places such as California, Germany, Japan and Australia. It's clear a critical piece of the jigsaw is missing, one that will help manage the volatile nature of the grid. This piece wasn't needed when everything was thermal, but renewables require us to up our game.
